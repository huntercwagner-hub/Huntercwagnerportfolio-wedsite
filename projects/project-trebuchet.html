Trebuchet Design & Structural Analysis

University of West Florida | Engineering Design Project

Project Overview

Objective: The goal of this project was to design, build, and test a fully functional trebuchet capable of launching a projectile over 250 yards. The challenge combined physics, structural mechanics, and practical engineering to create a precise and repeatable machine for long-range projectile motion.

Our team was tasked with developing a comprehensive design that incorporated analytical calculations, CAD modeling, and finite element analysis (FEA) to verify structural safety and performance. The entire build‚Äîfrom initial concept to successful test launches‚Äîwas completed within a one-month time frame.

Design and Development

As a member of the structure team, my role focused on the design, mathematical modeling, and CAD development of the trebuchet‚Äôs framework and throwing arm. I performed all structural load and stress calculations to ensure the frame and pivot assembly could safely support the counterweight and launch forces.

The design was developed using SolidWorks to create both detailed and assembly-level models. Every component‚Äîfrom the throwing arm to the base supports‚Äîwas dimensioned for manufacturability using standard wood sections. Structural integrity and performance were validated using ANSYS for static and dynamic loading scenarios.

  • Software Tools: SolidWorks (3D modeling), ANSYS (FEA analysis), Excel (data processing and equations)
  • Design Focus: Arm stiffness, pivot reaction forces, load path optimization, and stability under counterweight release
  • Construction: Wood and steel hybrid frame with adjustable counterweight attachment

Simulation and Ballistic Analysis

Using analytical methods, I modeled the trebuchet‚Äôs throwing motion to estimate launch velocity and projectile trajectory. These calculations formed the basis of a ballistic firing chart, predicting projectile range across various release angles and counterweight ratios.

The structure was validated through FEA simulations in ANSYS, ensuring the arm, axle supports, and base experienced stresses well below material limits. The Excel-based solver used during design was included within the final report to show parameter sensitivity for key variables such as arm ratio, sling length, and projectile mass.

Testing and Results

The completed trebuchet was tested under multiple loading conditions to measure range, accuracy, and structural behavior. Initial launches achieved distances approaching the 250-yard target, with minimal frame deflection and stable projectile release. The analytical predictions closely matched observed results, validating both the design and simulation models.

Throughout the project, rapid iteration and teamwork were key. The ability to integrate theoretical analysis, CAD design, and real-world testing allowed the team to meet tight deadlines and produce a reliable, high-performing mechanical system.
